Major Concepts to Understand

  • Currency Pairs: Currencies are traded in pairs like EUR/USD or GBP/JPY. The first currency is the base, the second is the quote.
  • Bid/Ask Spread: The difference between the buying (bid) and selling (ask) price. Tight spreads are better for traders.
  • Leverage: Allows traders to control large positions with small capital. Common ratios are 50:1 or 100:1, but leverage increases risk.
  • Market Hours: The forex market operates 24 hours a day, five days a week, across global financial centers.
  • Technical Analysis: Uses charts and indicators to predict price movements. Popular tools include RSI, MACD, and moving averages.
  • Fundamental Analysis: Focuses on economic data, interest rates, and geopolitical events to assess currency strength.
  • Risk Management: Includes stop-loss orders, position sizing, and avoiding over-leverage to protect capital.

Getting Started

To begin trading forex, choose a regulated broker, open a demo account, and study both technical and fundamental strategies. Practice discipline and always manage your risk.
