Glossary of Trading Terms

Ask Price
The lowest price a seller is willing to accept for a security.
Bid Price
The highest price a buyer is willing to pay for a security.
Leverage
Using borrowed capital to increase the potential return of an investment.
Liquidity
The ease with which an asset can be bought or sold without affecting its price.
Long Position
Buying a security with the expectation that its price will rise.
Margin
The amount of money required to open or maintain a leveraged position.
Order Types
Instructions to execute trades, such as market orders, limit orders, and stop orders.
Short Position
Selling a borrowed security with the expectation of buying it back at a lower price.
Spread
The difference between the bid and ask price of a security.
Stop Loss
An order placed to sell a security when it reaches a certain price to limit losses.
Support
A price level where a downtrend can be expected to pause due to demand.
Resistance
A price level where an uptrend can be expected to pause due to selling pressure.
Take Profit
An order to sell a security once it reaches a specified profit level.
Volume
The number of shares or contracts traded in a given period.
