What Is a Retail Trader?

A retail trader is an individual investor who buys and sells financial instruments using their own personal funds. Unlike institutional traders who manage large pools of capital for organizations, retail traders operate independently, often through online brokerage platforms. They participate in markets such as stocks, forex, cryptocurrencies, and commodities, typically trading smaller volumes. Retail traders may be full-time professionals, part-time enthusiasts, or beginners seeking to grow their wealth. With the rise of mobile apps and educational resources, retail trading has become more accessible. These traders rely on research, technical analysis, and market news to make informed decisions and manage risk.

Intro to Technical Analysis

Retail trading technical analysis involves evaluating price charts and market data to forecast future price movements. Retail traders use tools like candlestick patterns, moving averages, RSI, MACD, and support/resistance levels to identify entry and exit points. Unlike fundamental analysis, which focuses on company performance, technical analysis relies on historical price behavior and volume trends. Glossary of Trading Terms

Ask Price: The lowest price a seller is willing to accept.

Bid Price: The highest price a buyer is willing to pay.

Leverage: Using borrowed funds to increase position size.

Stop Loss: An order to limit losses by exiting a trade at a set price.
