Quick Reference: Most Used Technical Indicators

This guide summarizes the most popular technical indicators used by traders to analyze price action, momentum, volume, and volatility.

Indicator Category Purpose
Relative Strength Index (RSI) Momentum Measures overbought/oversold conditions; values above 70 suggest overbought, below 30 oversold.
Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) Momentum/Trend Shows trend direction and momentum using EMAs and signal line crossovers.
Simple Moving Average (SMA) Trend Calculates average price over a set period to smooth price action and identify trend direction.
Exponential Moving Average (EMA) Trend Similar to SMA but gives more weight to recent prices; reacts faster to price changes.
Bollinger Bands Volatility Plots bands two standard deviations from a moving average to identify volatility and price extremes.
Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP) Volume Shows average price weighted by volume; used by institutions to assess fair value.
Stochastic Oscillator Momentum Compares closing price to recent range to identify overbought/oversold conditions.
Average True Range (ATR) Volatility Measures market volatility by averaging true range over a period; higher ATR = more volatility.
On-Balance Volume (OBV) Volume Uses volume flow to predict price movement; rising OBV suggests accumulation.
Fibonacci Retracement Support/Resistance Identifies potential reversal levels based on Fibonacci ratios (23.6%, 38.2%, 61.8%).
